Frequently Asked Questions

Which Cree LED is best for high-output flashlights?

For maximum output in compact flashlights, the Cree XHP series (XHP35, XHP50, XHP70) offers the highest flux density. The XHP70.3 can achieve over 4000 lumens in a small 5mm² die, making it ideal for high-performance tactical and outdoor flashlights. For throw applications, pairing with smooth reflectors gives excellent results.

How do Cree LEDs compare to competitors in portable lighting?

Cree LEDs offer several advantages in portable lighting applications: superior flux density allowing for smaller optics, excellent thermal performance for sustained high output, and consistent binning for predictable performance. Cree's XLamp series is widely adopted in the portable lighting community for these reasons, with extensive third-party support for designs and accessories.

What are the key thermal design considerations?

In portable lighting, thermal management is critical due to size and weight constraints. Key considerations include: maximizing surface area for conduction and convection, using thermal interfaces with low resistance, implementing intelligent thermal regulation that adjusts output to maintain safe temperatures, and designing for the worst-case ambient temperature the device might encounter.
